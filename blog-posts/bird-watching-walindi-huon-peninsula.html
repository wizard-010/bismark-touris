Walindi Plantation Resort: Your Base for Huon Birding

Located just outside Kimbe in West New Britain, Walindi Plantation Resort has long been famous as Papua New Guinea's premier diving destination. Fewer travelers know that Walindi also operates exceptional bird watching safaris to the Huon Peninsula. With over 30 years of experience in Papua New Guinea's tourism industry, Walindi has developed the logistics, local relationships, and guide expertise to offer truly world-class birding experiences.

What makes Walindi's Huon Peninsula birding safaris so special is the seamless integration of comfort and adventure. Visitors stay in the resort's comfortable accommodation, with excellent food and facilities, while venturing into the field with expert guides who know the peninsula's birdlife intimately. The resort handles all logistics—flights, vehicle transfers, permits, and local guides—allowing guests to focus entirely on the birds.

The Huon Peninsula: A Center of Endemism

The Huon Peninsula is one of Papua New Guinea's most important centers of avian endemism. Its rugged mountains, which rise to over 4,000 meters, have isolated bird populations for millennia, resulting in a remarkable number of species found nowhere else on earth. For serious birders, the Huon is a must-visit destination—a place where multiple endemic species can be added to a life list in a single trip.

Key Endemic Species of the Huon Peninsula:

  • Huon Astrapia – The crown jewel of Huon birding. This spectacular Bird of Paradise, with its long, iridescent tail and emerald-green breast, is found only on the Huon Peninsula. Seeing one in its montane forest habitat is a life-list moment for any birder.
  • Wahnes's Parotia – Another Huon endemic, this Bird of Paradise performs an elaborate courtship dance on a cleared forest floor stage, displaying its iridescent throat feathers and unique head plumes.
  • Huon Melidectes – A striking honeyeater with bold facial markings, found only in the high-altitude forests of the Huon Peninsula.
  • Huon Bowerbird – A little-known bowerbird endemic to the peninsula, rarely seen but highly sought after by dedicated birders.

Other Notable Species in the Region:

  • Brehm's Tiger Parrot – A stunning parrot found in the hill forests of the Huon
  • Emperor Bird of Paradise – A spectacular species found in the lowland forests
  • Magnificent Bird of Paradise – Known for its incredible courtship display
  • MacGregor's Bird of Paradise – A high-altitude specialist found on the upper slopes
  • Eastern Riflebird – A beautiful species in the birds-of-paradise family
  • Papuan Hornbill – A large, noisy bird often seen in the lowlands

The Bird Watching Experience

A Huon Peninsula birding safari with Walindi Plantation Resort typically spans 5-7 days, allowing exploration of different altitude zones and targeting of the peninsula's endemic species. Here is what visitors can expect:

Lowland Forests (0-800m)

The lowland forests around the coast are rich with birdlife. Here, species such as the Papuan Hornbill, various fruit-doves, and the spectacular Emperor Bird of Paradise can be found. The early morning hours are best, with the forest coming alive with calls and activity.

Hill Forests (800-2,000m)

As elevation increases, the hill forest zone is reached, home to species like the Magnificent Bird of Paradise, Eastern Riflebird, and Brehm's Tiger Parrot. The trails here can be steep but are well-maintained, and the birding is exceptional.

Montane Forests (2,000m+)

The highest altitudes are where the Huon endemics are found: Huon Astrapia, Wahnes's Parotia, and Huon Melidectes. These forests are often shrouded in mist, creating a magical atmosphere. The birds here are adapted to cooler temperatures and are often remarkably approachable.

Combining Huon Birding with Goroka Show 2026

September is an excellent time for birding on the Huon Peninsula, with stable weather and active birds. It is also the month of the Goroka Show, offering a perfect opportunity to combine world-class bird watching with Papua New Guinea's premier cultural festival.

Planning a Walindi Huon Birding Safari

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (May through October) offers the most favorable conditions for bird watching on the Huon Peninsula. September is particularly ideal—the weather is stable, birds are active, and visits can be combined with the Goroka Show. The breeding season for most Birds of Paradise occurs between July and December, so September offers excellent opportunities to witness courtship displays.

Getting There

Walindi Plantation Resort is accessible via flights from Port Moresby to Hoskins Airport (Kimbe). From there, it is a short drive to the resort. For the Huon Peninsula birding safari, visitors take a charter flight or boat to the starting point, depending on the itinerary. All logistics are handled by Walindi.

Accommodation

Walindi Plantation Resort offers comfortable bungalow-style accommodation set in beautiful tropical gardens. Each bungalow features en-suite facilities, hot water, and comfortable beds. The resort's restaurant serves excellent local and international cuisine, and the bar is a great place to swap stories with fellow travelers after a day in the field.

During the Huon Peninsula safari portion, guests stay in comfortable guesthouses or basic eco-lodges that have been developed in partnership with local communities. These are simple but clean and comfortable, with a focus on the birding experience.

What to Bring

  • Binoculars – Essential for bird watching. High-quality optics recommended
  • Camera with telephoto lens – For capturing endemic species and landscapes
  • Warm layers – Higher elevations can be cool, especially in the morning
  • Waterproof jacket and pants – Mountain weather is unpredictable
  • Sturdy, broken-in hiking boots – Trails can be steep and muddy
  • Field guide – "Birds of New Guinea" by Pratt and Beehler is essential
  • Notebook and field checklist – For keeping a life list
  • Headlamp with extra batteries – For early morning departures
  • Insect repellent – Especially for lowland areas
  • Reusable water bottle – Staying hydrated is important on long walks

Conservation and Community

The forests of the Huon Peninsula are under pressure from logging and agriculture. Bird watching tourism provides a powerful economic incentive for conservation. When visitors choose Walindi and Bismark Touris, they directly support local communities who have chosen to protect their forests rather than clear them. Their visit helps ensure that these unique birds will survive for generations to come.

Strict ethical bird watching guidelines are practiced: maintaining respectful distances, limiting group sizes, and never using playback calls during the breeding season. Guides are trained in sustainable birding practices and are passionate about conservation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many endemic species can be expected on the Huon Peninsula?

With a dedicated 5-day birding safari, visitors have excellent chances of seeing the Huon Astrapia, Wahnes's Parotia, and Huon Melidectes. The Huon Bowerbird is more elusive, but sightings are possible with patience and experienced guides.

Is the Huon Peninsula birding suitable for beginners?

While some of the trails are challenging, the experience can be tailored to individual fitness and experience levels. The Huon Peninsula offers both easier lowland birding and more strenuous montane hikes. Discussing interests and abilities when booking is recommended.

What is the photography like on the Huon Peninsula?

The Huon Peninsula offers exceptional bird photography opportunities, particularly for the endemic Birds of Paradise. Guides know the best display sites and can position photographers for optimal lighting. A telephoto lens (400mm or longer) is recommended.

Can Huon birding be combined with other destinations?

Absolutely. The most popular combination is with the Goroka Show, as both are best visited in September. Combinations with Mount Wilhelm trekking, Tari Valley birding, and coastal diving at Walindi are also available.

What is the cost of a Huon Peninsula birding safari?

Prices vary based on group size, number of days, and whether combined with the Goroka Show. A 7-day Huon birding safari typically starts from PGK 4,500 per person (based on 2 people sharing). Contact Bismark Touris for a personalized quote.
