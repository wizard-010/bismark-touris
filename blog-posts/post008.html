The announcement came quietly, passed from the Eastern Highlands Provincial Government to the Papua New Guinea Tourism Promotion Authority, then whispered through the network of tour operators, guides, and cultural enthusiasts who have been waiting with bated breath. The Goroka Show — the oldest and most celebrated tribal gathering in the Pacific — will return to its ancestral home from September 11 to 13, 2026.

I have been to the Goroka Show more times than I can count. I have stood on the edge of the main arena as the sun rose over the misty highlands, watching as hundreds of tribes emerged from the morning fog like spirits returning to the world of the living. I have felt the ground tremble beneath the synchronized stamping of a thousand feet, the air thick with the scent of ochre and pig grease and the sweet smoke of ceremonial fires. I have seen the Asaro Mudmen rise from the earth, their ghostly clay masks sending shivers down my spine even after all these years.

And every year, I meet travelers from every corner of the globe — photographers with lenses longer than my arm, anthropologists with notebooks filled with careful observations, young backpackers who saw a photograph somewhere and decided they had to be here, retirees who have been dreaming of this moment for decades. They come to Goroka for the same reason. They come to witness something that cannot be found anywhere else on earth.

In this guide, I will tell you everything you need to know about the Goroka Show 2026. The dates, the history, the tribes, the logistics, the hidden secrets that only those who have walked the dusty streets of Goroka in September truly understand. By the time you finish reading, you will have a map not just of the festival, but of the journey — the journey that will bring you to the heart of Papua New Guinea's highlands, where ancient traditions meet the modern world in a celebration of color, sound, and spirit.

Confirmed Dates for Goroka Show 2026

Let me give you the most important piece of information first. The Goroka Show 2026 will be held from September 11 to 13, 2026 (Friday through Sunday). The main arena events take place each day from approximately 9:00 AM until 4:00 PM, though the energy of the festival spills out into the streets, the market, and the guesthouses long before the official start and after the last dance has ended.

These dates are confirmed by the Goroka Show Committee and the Eastern Highlands Provincial Government. I have verified them personally with my contacts in Goroka — people who have been involved with the show for decades, who remember when it was a small gathering of a few villages and has now grown into the largest cultural festival in Papua New Guinea.

If you are planning to attend, I urge you to start making arrangements immediately. Accommodation in Goroka books out months in advance — sometimes a full year ahead. Flights into Goroka Airport (GKA) become scarce as the date approaches, and prices rise accordingly. This is not a festival you can decide to attend on a whim. It requires planning, preparation, and patience. But I promise you, every bit of effort is rewarded a thousand times over.

A Brief History: The Birth of a National Treasure

To understand the Goroka Show, you must understand where it came from. The first Goroka Show was held in 1957, a time when Papua New Guinea was still under Australian administration. It was called the Goroka Agricultural Show, modeled after the agricultural shows of rural Australia — a way to bring together the diverse peoples of the Eastern Highlands to display their crops, livestock, and crafts.

But something unexpected happened. The people who came to the show brought more than their sweet potatoes and their pigs. They brought their traditions. They brought their songs, their dances, their body paint, their feathers, their stories. They came in their ceremonial regalia, and when they performed, the crowd fell silent, then cheered, then wept.

What began as an agricultural show became something far greater. It became a celebration of culture, a gathering of tribes who had, for centuries, been separated by mountains and language and the old ways of warfare. Here, in the dusty arena of Goroka, they found a place to come together. Not to fight, but to dance. Not to compete in battle, but to compete in beauty, in artistry, in the preservation of traditions that had been passed down through countless generations.

The Tribes: A Mosaic of Highlands Cultures

To tell you about the tribes of the Goroka Show is to tell you about Papua New Guinea itself. This is a country of over 800 languages, of thousands of clans, of cultures that have evolved in isolation for millennia. The highlands, where Goroka sits, are the most densely populated and culturally diverse region of the country.

  • The Asaro Mudmen: Perhaps the most famous of all PNG tribal performers. The Mudmen come from the Asaro Valley, just west of Goroka. Their story is legendary: during a conflict with a neighboring tribe, the Asaro warriors fled into the Asaro River. They emerged at night, covered in gray mud, their bodies ghostly in the darkness. Their enemies, believing they were spirits, fled in terror.
  • The Huli Wigmen: From the Southern Highlands, the Huli are famous for their elaborate wigs created from human hair grown and shaped over years, decorated with feathers of birds of paradise, cuscus fur, and bright flowers.
  • The Chimbu Skeleton Dancers: From Simbu Province, the Skeleton Dancers paint their bodies with white clay in patterns that mimic the bones of the human body, telling stories of death and resurrection.
  • The Eastern Highlands Groups: Local tribes — the Fore, the Kamano, the Benabena, the Gahuku-Gama — perform with vibrant body paint and massive headdresses.

What to Expect: A Day at the Goroka Show

5:30 AM — The Town Wakes
Goroka sits at 1,600 meters above sea level. By 5:30, the town is stirring with trucks and buses bringing performers to the showground.

7:00 AM — Arrival at the Showground
The gates open early. Arrive by 7:00 AM to secure a good spot. Grandstand seating fills quickly.

9:00 AM — The Grand Parade
All 100+ tribes enter the arena together — a flood of feathers, paint, drums, and song.

10:00 AM to 4:00 PM — Group Performances
Each group performs for 10 to 15 minutes. The schedule is fluid — embrace Papua New Guinea time.

12:00 PM — Lunch
Try traditional mumu (pork and vegetables cooked in an earth oven), kaukau (sweet potato), or buai (betel nut) if you are adventurous.

4:00 PM — The Closing Parade
Tribes gather one last time. This is your chance to meet performers, ask questions, and take photos with permission.

Traveling to Goroka: Getting There

By Air: Air Niugini and PNG Air operate daily flights from Port Moresby (POM) to Goroka Airport (GKA). Flight time is about one hour. Book at least three to six months in advance for the Goroka Show.

By Road: The Highlands Highway from Lae to Goroka takes 6–8 hours. Recommended only for experienced travelers with local guides.

By Tour Group: Bismark Touris offers Goroka Show packages including flights, accommodation, meals, and guided access.

Accommodation: Where to Stay

This is the most critical logistical consideration. Goroka is a small town with limited accommodation. Book now for September 2026.

Main Hotels: Bird of Paradise Hotel, Pacific Gardens Hotel, Goroka Hotel.
Guesthouses: Rainbow Lodge, Mala Lodge, Lopi Lodge.
Alternative: Some visitors stay in Mount Hagen (2.5 hours away) and travel to Goroka daily.

Practical Tips for Attendees

  • Bring cash — ATMs run out during the show. Bring enough PGK kina.
  • Dress in layers — Warm days (25°C) but cool mornings (12°C).
  • Protect from the sun — Hat, sunglasses, sunscreen.
  • Stay hydrated — Drink bottled water.
  • Ask before taking photos — Always ask permission. Offer 5–10 PGK for close-up portraits.
  • Secure belongings — Use a money belt or secure bag.
  • Be respectful — This is a sacred cultural gathering, not a theme park.

Beyond the Show: Exploring Eastern Highlands

The Asaro Mudmen Village: About 30 minutes from Goroka, visit the village where Mudmen masks are made and learn the history firsthand.

J.K. McCarthy Museum: Excellent collection of highlands artifacts and photographs from the early days of contact.

Daulo Pass: Spectacular views at over 2,500 meters. Site of a WWII battle.

Coffee Plantations: Tour local coffee plantations and taste Eastern Highlands' famous Arabica coffee.

A Cultural Reflection: The Meaning of the Goroka Show

Papua New Guinea is a country in transition. The old ways are changing. But in Goroka, in September, the old Papua New Guinea comes alive. For three days, the ancestors walk the earth again. The languages that are fading are sung in the arena. The traditions that are being lost are danced in the dust.

That is what the Goroka Show is. It is a remembering. A gathering of a people who have called these mountains home for thousands of years. It is a declaration that we are still here, that our cultures are still alive.

When you come to the Goroka Show, you are not just a spectator. You are a witness. You become part of the remembering. I hope you will come. I hope you will stand in the cool morning mist and watch as the first tribe enters the arena. I hope you will leave changed, carrying a piece of Papua New Guinea with you.

Frequently Asked Questions: Goroka Show 2026

What are the official Goroka Show 2026 dates?

The Goroka Show 2026 will be held from September 11 to 13, 2026 (Friday through Sunday). The main arena events run from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM daily.

How much do Goroka Show tickets cost?

General admission is approximately 50 PGK per day (about $14 USD). Grandstand seating is 100–150 PGK per day and should be booked in advance through tour operators.

Can I take photos at the Goroka Show?

Yes, but always ask permission before photographing performers. A small gratuity of 5–10 PGK is customary for close-up portraits. Never photograph without consent.

How do I get to Goroka for the show?

Fly from Port Moresby (POM) to Goroka Airport (GKA) with Air Niugini or PNG Air. Book flights 3–6 months in advance. Organized tours from Bismark Touris include all transport.

Is accommodation available in Goroka?

Accommodation is extremely limited during the show. Book hotels or guesthouses at least 6–8 months ahead. Bismark Touris can assist with bookings.

What should I pack for the Goroka Show?

Pack layers — warm jacket for cool mornings (12°C/54°F), light clothing for warm afternoons (25°C/77°F). Comfortable walking shoes, hat, sunscreen, sunglasses, and a reusable water bottle.
