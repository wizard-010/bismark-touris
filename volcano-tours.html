Guided Volcano Tours in Papua New Guinea

Hike to the rim of Mt. Tavurvur, soak in volcanic hot springs, and explore the dramatic volcanic landscape of East New Britain.

Experience the Power of Nature

East New Britain is one of the most volcanically active regions in the world, offering visitors a rare opportunity to witness the raw power of nature up close. Our guided volcano tours take you to the legendary Mt. Tavurvur, which famously erupted in 1994 and continues to emit steam and ash today. Led by experienced local guides who understand the volcanic landscape and its history, these tours are both thrilling and educational.

Whether you're a geology enthusiast, adventure seeker, or simply curious about the forces that shape our planet, our volcano tours offer an unforgettable experience. The views from the crater rim stretch across Rabaul Harbour, Simpson Harbour, and the surrounding volcanoes—a panorama that will stay with you forever.

Sample Itinerary: Half-Day Mt. Tavurvur Hike

7:00 AM
Pick-up from your hotel in Kokopo or Rabaul. Meet your guide and receive a safety briefing.
7:30 AM
Drive to the base of Mt. Tavurvur. The drive takes about 30 minutes along the scenic coastline with views of the caldera.
8:00 AM
Begin the hike. The trail climbs steadily through volcanic ash and scrub. The hike takes approximately 1.5-2 hours depending on fitness level. Your guide will point out geological features and explain the volcano's history.
9:30 AM
Reach the crater rim. Take in breathtaking views of Rabaul Harbour, Simpson Harbour, and the surrounding volcanoes. Witness steam vents and sulfur deposits. Your guide will explain the geology and the 1994 eruption.
10:30 AM
Descend back to the trailhead, taking a different route for varied perspectives.
11:30 AM
Return to your hotel with memories to last a lifetime.

Essential Information

What to Bring

  • Sturdy closed-toe shoes with good grip (hiking boots recommended)
  • Lightweight, breathable clothing (long pants recommended for ash protection)
  • Sun hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen (high altitude sun is intense)
  • At least 1.5 liters of water per person
  • Camera with dust protection (volcanic ash can be abrasive)
  • Light rain jacket (weather in the Highlands can change quickly)
  • Small backpack to carry your belongings

Health & Fitness Requirements

  • The Mt. Tavurvur hike is considered moderate difficulty with steep sections
  • Good cardiovascular fitness is recommended
  • The trail surface is loose volcanic ash and rock—hiking poles are available upon request
  • Please inform us of any medical conditions or mobility concerns before booking
  • Travel insurance covering adventure activities is strongly recommended

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mt. Tavurvur currently active?

Yes, Mt. Tavurvur remains active with continuous steam and occasional ash emissions. We monitor activity levels daily and only conduct tours when conditions are safe. The Volcanological Observatory provides real-time data that guides our operations.

Is the hike suitable for beginners?

The hike is considered moderate. While beginners can complete it with determination, a reasonable level of fitness is recommended. The trail has some steep sections and the volcanic ash surface can be slippery. We're happy to discuss your fitness level before booking.

What happens if the volcano becomes more active?

Safety is our absolute priority. If volcanic activity increases to unsafe levels, tours are cancelled and full refunds are provided. We maintain constant communication with the Volcanological Observatory and follow their recommendations.

Can I visit the hot springs year-round?

Yes, the hot springs are accessible year-round. Water temperatures vary by pool, ranging from warm to hot. Towels are provided, and changing facilities are available. The experience is especially pleasant during the drier months (May-October).
